About Me

Hi! I'm a rising junior at Bowdoin College in Brunswick, Maine, majoring in Computer Science and Mathematics and minoring in psychology. I am broadly interested in the intersections of computation, language, and cognition, and am currently most passionate about advances in multimodal Natural Language Processing (NLP). As a Gibbons Research Fellow with the Lexicon Lab at Bowdoin, I investigated age-related differences in semantic fluency using computational modeling, which culminated in a poster presentation at the Psychonomic Society's Annual Conference in New York. I lead the Quiz Bowl team at Bowdoin and work at the Baldwin Center for Learning and Teaching as a Learning Assistant and Quantitative Tutor.

We used a random walk model on a semantic network constructed from human word association data to simulate the semantic fluency task, in which participants name as many items as possible from a given category (e.g., animals) in a limited time. Our model was able to replicate key patterns observed in human data, such as the number of words produced and the clustering of related words, and we found that older adults' performance could be explained by a smaller and less connected semantic network. This work culminated in a poster presentation at the Psychonomic Society's Annual Conference in New York.
